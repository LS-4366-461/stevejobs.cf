Steve Jobs

From an orphan and college dropout, to a successful billionaire.
The making of the Apple® and Pixar®, and the forced resignation of Apple's most profitable CEO.

Early Life

I am an orphan born in Palo Alto California, where I first met Steve Wozniak my best friend, and partner in trade.
He and I worked in my garage a lot of times, even though I often wanted an upgrade.
After a while we found the computer, which Woz loved, and we thought it was not well done.
I went to an Apple Orchard over the weekend and then proceeded to invite Woz to come make a company with me, Apple.
He refused but later I got him on board.

Apple

We started apple with an idea that did not always seem like it would come through but it did. After I left college I focused with Wozniak on the computers, we made the Apple and Apple II. The Apple was the first one, we started off with $1,300 and we sold the first one at $666, we showed it off at a convention and we sold many more, bringing in $774,000, in 1974. By 1976 we had already made $13,900,000 on selling computers. Another computer was the Mac, a computer for the people in 1984, it was 32-bit while the others were 16-bit. I took a break from the company from 1985 to 1996, but in 2001 we started the iPod series. In 2007 we started the iPhone series, then I named Tim Cook CEO after I rejoined Apple at $1 per year.

Pixar & Next

While I was taking my break in 1985, I was busy. In 1986 I bought Pixar Animation Studios, and made it a successful company. We produced movies like ToyStory and Monsters Inc., which are actually all based around Apple and me. Pixar was sold to Walt Disney.
NeXT was a company that was designed to be a software company, it didn't go terribly well.
